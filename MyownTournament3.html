1930 World Cup Venezuela

The first world cup took place in the South American continent in the country of Venezuela. In this tournament, 16 teams participated in which 6 teams were from South America, 6 from Europe, and 4 from North America. This tournament brought numerous surprises as well as disappointments. Throughout the cup, the North American teams seemed to dominate the tournament since all of the four teams end up being in the top 8. The final was between the host country and Mexico. The game ended in a 1-1 draw, and the game was decided in a penalty shootout. Mexico beat Venezuela 3-1 and became the first world champions in history.

Unfortunately, the host disappoinnted everyone after they didn't pass to the quarter-finals. The other European teams were also expected to do a an amazing performance in the cup, but most did poorly. The tournament was onced more dominated by the North American teams in which two of them faced themselves in the final: Mexico and Honduras. The defending champions, Mexico, was once again in the final and had to face a stong honduras team which have surpried many in this cup. The final ended in a 3-2 victory for Honduras and they became the second world champions and the second North American team to win the world cup.
